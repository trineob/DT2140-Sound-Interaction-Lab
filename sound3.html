    • Our sonic interaction design here!

    In this project, each sound is connected to a different gesture. The torpedo sound is triggered when the user rotates the phone, similar to swinging a sword. The sound activates when the device is rotated roughly between 90° and 120°. The church bell sound is produced when the user shakes the phone, mimicking the action of ringing a bell. Finally, the engine sound is linked to movement: when the phone is moved, it behaves like starting a car engine as the “car” (the phone) begins to move.

    Developed by Maurizio Berta and Laura McHugh
    for DT2140 Multimodal Interfaces Sound Interaction Lab
